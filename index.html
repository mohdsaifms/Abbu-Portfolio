About me

I'm Dr. Noor Mohammad and I'm a

I have extensive experience in electoral management and democratic governance. As a voting member of Afghanistan's Joint Electoral Management Body (JEMB) in 2005, I played a key role in drafting electoral law amendments and capacity-building for local staff, contributing to the successful parliamentary and provincial council elections. As Chief Electoral Advisor to the Independent Election Commission (IEC) of Afghanistan under a UNDP contract, I led international advisors in enhancing the IEC's capabilities, developed civil and voter registry procedures, and secured UNDEF funding for a pilot CVR project. My election observation experience includes Mexican (2003), Australian (2004), and Nigerian (2007) elections. As a member of the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) since 1977, I have led projects in voter registration and election management, including computerizing voter registers for Kashmir and conducting five General Elections in India. I hold a PhD in Economics and am currently a faculty member at the India International Institute of Democracy and Electoral Management, New Delhi. My expertise in international and domestic electoral processes, combined with my leadership skills, positions me well for any election-related assignment.
